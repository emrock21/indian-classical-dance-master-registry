# Indian Classical Dance Master Registry

This registry documents the regional schools, lineages, and technical traditions of Indian classical dance.  
Indian classical dance is a single master tradition with deep internal diversity: styles, banis/gharanas, pedagogy, repertoire, and aesthetics vary across regions and lineages.

## Purpose

The goal of this contract is to preserve authentic dance knowledge in a permanent, verifiable, and censorship‑resistant format.  
Each entry records a real school, lineage, or style, including:

- region  
- lineage or school  
- style name  
- materials (costume, jewelry, instruments)  
- technique (footwork, mudras, abhinaya, posture)  
- repertoire (core forms and compositions)  
- cultural uniqueness  

## Why this registry matters

Indian classical dance encompasses multiple major styles:

- Bharatanatyam (Tamil Nadu)  
- Kathak (North India)  
- Odissi (Odisha)  
- Kuchipudi (Andhra Pradesh)  
- Kathakali (Kerala)  
- Manipuri (Manipur)  
- Mohiniyattam (Kerala)  
- Sattriya (Assam)  

Within each style, distinct schools and lineages preserve specific techniques, repertoires, and aesthetics.  
By recording these variations on‑chain, the registry protects cultural memory and supports future research, pedagogy, and artistic revival.
